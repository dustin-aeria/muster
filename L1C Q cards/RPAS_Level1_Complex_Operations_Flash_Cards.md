# RPAS Level 1 Complex Operations - Comprehensive Study Flash Cards
## Canadian Aviation Regulations (SOR/96-433)
### AUDITED & CORRECTED VERSION

---

# SECTION 1: DEFINITIONS

---

### Q1: What is BVLOS?
**A:** Beyond Visual Line-of-Sight - operating a remotely piloted aircraft outside the pilot's unaided visual range.

---

### Q2: What is VLOS?
**A:** Visual Line-of-Sight - unaided visual contact maintained with a remotely piloted aircraft sufficient to:
- Maintain control
- Know location
- Detect conflicting traffic and hazards

---

### Q3: What is Extended VLOS?
**A:** An operation where the aircraft is NOT in visual line-of-sight, but unaided visual contact IS maintained with the **airspace** to detect conflicts and take avoidance action.

---

### Q4: What is a Sheltered Operation?
**A:** An operation where the aircraft:
- Remains **less than 200 feet (61 m) horizontally** from a building/structure
- At an altitude **no greater than 100 feet (30 m)**

---

### Q5: What is Flight Geography?
**A:** The area within which a remotely piloted aircraft is **intended to fly** for a specific operation.

---

### Q6: What is Contingency Volume?
**A:** The area **immediately surrounding the flight geography** within which contingency procedures are intended to be used to:
- Return the aircraft to flight geography, OR
- Safely terminate the flight

---

### Q7: What is Ground Risk Buffer?
**A:** The area immediately surrounding the contingency volume that, when measured horizontally from the perimeter of the contingency volume, is **at least equal to the planned maximum altitude**.

---

### Q8: What is Operational Volume?
**A:** The combined area composed of:
1. Flight Geography
2. Contingency Volume
3. Ground Risk Buffer

---

### Q9: What is a Small Remotely Piloted Aircraft?
**A:** Operating weight of **at least 250g but not more than 25 kg (55 lbs)**.

---

### Q10: What is a Medium Remotely Piloted Aircraft?
**A:** Operating weight of **more than 25 kg (55 lbs) but not more than 150 kg (331 lbs)**.

---

### Q11: What is Operating Weight?
**A:** The weight of the aircraft at any point during flight, including:
- Any payload
- Any safety equipment aboard or connected

---

### Q12: What is a Populated Area?
**A:** An area with **more than 5 people per square kilometre**.

---

### Q13: What is a Sparsely Populated Area?
**A:** An area with **more than 5 but not more than 25 people per square kilometre**.

---

### Q14: What is a Payload?
**A:** A system, object, or collection of objects (including slung loads) that is on board or connected to the aircraft but is **NOT required for flight**.

---

### Q15: What is a Visual Observer?
**A:** A crew member who is **trained to assist the pilot** in ensuring the safe conduct of a flight.

---

### Q16: What is a Fly-away?
**A:** When there is an **interruption or loss of the command and control link** such that the pilot is **no longer able to control** the aircraft.

---

### Q17: What are Contingency Procedures?
**A:** Procedures to be followed to address conditions that **could lead to an unsafe situation**.

---

# SECTION 2: LEVEL 1 COMPLEX OPERATIONS - SCOPE (901.87)

---

### Q18: What two categories of BVLOS operations does Level 1 Complex (Division VI) cover?
**A:**

**Category (a) - Small OR Medium RPAS:**
- BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
- **At least 1 km from a populated area** (MANDATORY)

**Category (b) - Small RPAS ONLY:**
- BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
- Over a **sparsely populated area**, OR
- **Less than 1 km** from a populated area

---

### Q19: Can MEDIUM RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area?
**A:** **NO** - Medium RPAS can ONLY conduct BVLOS under Category (a), which requires a minimum distance of **1 km from populated areas**. Category (b) allowing closer operations is restricted to **small RPAS only**.

---

### Q20: Can SMALL RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area?
**A:** **YES** - Small RPAS can operate under Category (b), which permits BVLOS:
- Over sparsely populated areas, OR
- At distances **less than 1 km** from populated areas

---

### Q21: What is the key difference between small and medium RPAS for L1C BVLOS operations?
**A:**
- **Medium RPAS**: Must ALWAYS maintain **at least 1 km** from populated areas (Category a only)
- **Small RPAS**: Can operate at **1+ km** (Category a) OR **closer than 1 km/over sparsely populated areas** (Category b)

---

### Q22: What airspace type is Level 1 Complex Operations restricted to?
**A:** **Uncontrolled airspace ONLY** - controlled airspace operations require additional certifications and ATC coordination.

---

### Q23: Can you conduct Level 1 Complex Operations in controlled airspace?
**A:** **NO** - Level 1 Complex is restricted to uncontrolled airspace. Controlled airspace requires additional authorization.

---

### Q24: Where can small RPAS conduct BVLOS under Category (b)?
**A:** In uncontrolled airspace:
- Over **sparsely populated areas** (5-25 people/km²), OR
- At a distance of **less than 1 km** from a populated area

---

# SECTION 3: PILOT CERTIFICATE REQUIREMENTS

---

### Q25: What is the minimum age for a Level 1 Complex Operations pilot certificate?
**A:** **18 years old**

---

### Q26: What are the requirements for a Level 1 Complex pilot certificate?
**A:**
1. Be at least **18 years old**
2. Successfully complete the **Advanced Operations examination** (TP 15263)
3. Complete **20 hours of RPAS ground school instruction** from a training provider covering L1C operations (TP 15530)
4. Pass the **Level 1 Complex Operations examination** (TP 15530)
5. Complete **flight review within 12 months** prior to application (Standard 921)

---

### Q27: What is the minimum age for a Basic Operations certificate?
**A:** **14 years old**

---

### Q28: What is the minimum age for an Advanced Operations certificate?
**A:** **16 years old**

---

### Q29: What ground school hours are required for Level 1 Complex?
**A:** **20 hours** of RPAS ground school instruction delivered by an approved training provider, covering subjects in TP 15530.

---

### Q30: What examination must be passed BEFORE the L1C exam?
**A:** The **Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems - Advanced Operations** examination (TP 15263).

---

### Q31: What Transport Canada documents define L1C knowledge requirements?
**A:**
- **TP 15263**: Basic and Advanced Operations knowledge requirements
- **TP 15530**: Level 1 Complex Operations knowledge requirements

---

# SECTION 4: RECENCY REQUIREMENTS

---

### Q32: What is the recency period for Level 1 Complex Operations?
**A:** **24 months** - must have completed qualifying requirements within 24 months before operating.

---

### Q33: What qualifies for recency under Level 1 Complex Operations?
**A:** Within 24 months, must have completed ONE of:
- Issuance of the certificate, OR
- Basic, Advanced, or L1C examinations, OR
- Flight reviews per Standard 921, OR
- Recurrent training activities per section 921.04 of Standard 921

---

### Q34: How long must pilots retain recency activity records?
**A:** **At least 24 months** after the day on which they were completed.

---

### Q35: What must be easily accessible during flight operations?
**A:**
- Pilot certificate
- Proof of recency compliance
- Authorization documents (if applicable)
- Flight review completion records

---

# SECTION 5: FLIGHT REVIEW & EXAMINATION RULES

---

### Q36: How long before application must the flight review be completed?
**A:** **Within 12 months** prior to certificate application.

---

### Q37: What is the waiting period to retake a failed examination?
**A:** **24 hours** before retaking failed examinations or flight reviews.

---

### Q38: What is prohibited during examinations?
**A:**
- Cheating
- Unauthorized assistance
- Possession of unauthorized materials

---

### Q39: What standard governs flight reviews for RPAS pilots?
**A:** **Standard 921 - Remotely Piloted Aircraft**, specifically section 921.02.

---

# SECTION 6: RPAS OPERATOR CERTIFICATE

---

### Q40: Is an RPAS Operator Certificate required for Level 1 Complex Operations?
**A:** **YES** - mandatory for conducting Level 1 Complex Operations.

---

### Q41: Who can conduct Level 1 Complex Operations?
**A:** A person who:
- IS an RPAS operator, OR
- Is an employee, agent, mandatary, or representative of an RPAS operator

**AND** complies with the conditions in the RPAS operator certificate.

---

### Q42: Who is eligible for an RPAS Operator Certificate (commercial)?
**A:** Must be **Canadian** (Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or Canadian-incorporated entity).

---

### Q43: Who is eligible for an RPAS Operator Certificate (non-commercial)?
**A:**
- Canadian citizens or permanent residents
- Government entities in Canada (or their agents/representatives)
- Corporations incorporated under Canadian federal or provincial law

---

### Q44: What information is required for Operator Certificate application?
**A:**
- Legal name and contact information
- Accountable executive identification
- Declaration of operations manual compliance
- Training program compliance
- Operational intent statement

---

# SECTION 7: RPAS DECLARATION REQUIREMENTS (901.194)

---

### Q45: What must be submitted in an RPAS Declaration?
**A:**
1. Legal name, trade name, address, contact details
2. **Model name** of the RPAS
3. **Configurable elements** of the system
4. **Intended operations** to be conducted
5. Technical requirements from **Standard 922** subject to declaration

---

### Q46: What makes an RPAS Declaration invalid?
**A:** Three conditions:
1. The model **fails to meet** specified technical requirements
2. The declarant **notifies the Minister** of invalidity
3. **Annual reporting requirements** are not met

---

### Q47: What document is required before making a declaration for certain advanced BVLOS operations?
**A:** An **acceptance letter** must have been issued **within 2 years prior** to making the declaration.

---

### Q48: Within what timeframe must declarants notify the Minister of contact changes?
**A:** **Within 30 days** of any changes to names, addresses, or contact information.

---

### Q49: What must be included in annual reports?
**A:**
- Operator name
- Aircraft model name
- Operational hours in Canada
- Count of service difficulty reports with summaries of mandatory actions

---

### Q50: Is a declaration required before conducting L1C operations?
**A:** **YES** - Per section 901.95, no pilot shall operate under Division VI (L1C) unless a declaration has been made in accordance with section 901.194.

---

### Q51: What must the Operations Manual describe?

### Q52: What documentation must operators provide to users?

- Establish Maintenance Control Manuals

- Establish procedures consistent with manufacturer instructions

**A:**
- Crew involvement records

### Q58: How long must operator records be maintained?

# SECTION 12: GENERAL OPERATING RULES

---

### Q59: What is the standard maximum altitude for RPAS operations?
**A:** **400 feet (122 m) AGL**

---

### Q60: What altitude restriction applies when within 200 feet horizontally of a structure?
**A:** Must maintain altitude **no greater than 100 feet (30 m) above** any building or structure.

---

### Q61: What is the minimum horizontal distance from uninvolved persons for SMALL RPAS (VLOS)?
**A:** **100 feet (30 m)** measured horizontally at any altitude.

---

### Q62: What is the minimum horizontal distance from uninvolved persons for MEDIUM RPAS (VLOS)?
**A:** **500 feet (152.4 m)** measured horizontally at any altitude.

---

### Q63: What must pilots yield to?
**A:** Must give way to:
- Power-driven heavier-than-air aircraft
- Airships
- Gliders
- Balloons

**AT ALL TIMES**

---

### Q64: What is the alcohol restriction for RPAS crew members?
**A:** Cannot serve:
- Within **12 hours** after consuming alcohol
- While under alcohol's influence
- While using impairing drugs

---

### Q65: When must operations immediately cease?
**A:** If aviation safety or the safety of any person is **endangered or likely to be endangered**.

---

### Q66: What must happen if an aircraft loses pilot control and enters/threatens controlled airspace?
**A:** **Immediately notify** the appropriate ATS unit or agency.

---

### Q67: Can RPAS carry passengers?
**A:** **NO** - except with a special flight operations certificate issued under section 903.03.

---

### Q68: Can RPAS leave Canadian Domestic Airspace?
**A:** **NO** - except with a special flight operations certificate.

---

# SECTION 13: VISUAL OBSERVER REQUIREMENTS

---

### Q69: What communication must be maintained between pilot and visual observer?
**A:** **Reliable and timely communication** during the operation.

---

### Q70: Can a visual observer monitor multiple aircraft?
**A:** **NO** - cannot monitor multiple aircraft simultaneously (except under Division V conditions or special certificates).

---

### Q71: Can visual observers perform duties while operating vehicles?
**A:** **NO** - cannot perform duties while operating vehicles, vessels, or aircraft.

---

### Q72: What is the visual observer's reporting obligation?
**A:** Must **promptly communicate** detection of conflicting traffic or hazards to the pilot.

---

### Q73: What certificates must visual observers hold?
**A:** Must hold one of:
- Small RPAS basic certificate
- Remotely piloted aircraft advanced certificate
- Level 1 Complex Operations certificate

---

### Q74: What is the maximum distance a visual observer can be from the aircraft?
**A:** Must maintain visual contact **within 2 nautical miles** of the aircraft.

---

# SECTION 14: PRE-FLIGHT REQUIREMENTS

---

### Q75: What must be determined in a site survey?
**A:**
- Airspace type and requirements
- Altitudes and routes for approach/launch/recovery
- Proximity of other aircraft operations
- Distance from airports and aerodromes
- Obstacles (wires, towers, buildings, turbines)
- Weather and environmental conditions
- Horizontal distance from uninvolved persons
- Distance from populated areas (BVLOS)

---

### Q76: What must pilots be familiar with before flight?
**A:**
- Results of site survey
- Declarations regarding aircraft systems
- Crew qualifications

---

### Q77: What emergency procedures must be established?
**A:**
- Control station failure
- Equipment failure
- Aircraft failure
- Loss of command and control link
- Fly-away scenarios
- Flight termination
- Detection and avoidance of conflicting traffic

---

# SECTION 15: CONTROLLED AIRSPACE & AIRPORT PROXIMITY

---

### Q78: What is required for controlled airspace operations?
**A:** Authorization from air traffic services provider.

---

### Q79: What information must be provided for controlled airspace authorization?
**A:**
- Operation date, time, duration
- Aircraft category, registration, characteristics
- Operational boundaries and altitude limits
- Pilot contact information and certificate number
- Emergency procedures
- Lost command/control link procedures

---

### Q80: What distance restriction applies near airports?
**A:** Cannot operate within **3 nautical miles** of airport centers without established safe-use procedures.

---

### Q81: What distance restriction applies near heliports?
**A:** Cannot operate within **1 nautical mile** of heliport centers without established safe-use procedures.

---

# SECTION 16: EXTENDED VLOS & SHELTERED OPERATIONS

---

### Q82: What are the requirements for Extended VLOS operations?
**A:**
- Pilot and control station at take-off/landing site during those activities
- Aircraft within **2 nautical miles** of pilot/observer
- Minimum **100 feet horizontal** from uninvolved persons
- Visual observer must maintain unaided visual contact to detect hazards

---

# SECTION 17: REGISTRATION & NOTIFICATION

---

### Q83: What must be easily accessible during operations regarding registration?
**A:** The **certificate of registration** issued for the remotely piloted aircraft.

---

### Q84: Within what timeframe must owners notify the Minister of aircraft changes?
**A:** **Within 7 days** of:
- Aircraft destruction
- Permanent withdrawal from use
- Aircraft missing with terminated search
- Aircraft missing 60+ days
- Transfer of custody and control

---

# SECTION 18: SERVICE DIFFICULTY REPORTING

---

### Q85: What must operators establish for service difficulties?
**A:** Systems to **receive, record, analyze, and investigate** reports concerning reportable service difficulties.

---

### Q86: What action must be taken when deficiencies are discovered?
**A:** Must **develop a mandatory action to rectify the deficiency**.

---

# SECTION 19: ADVANCED OPERATIONS (901.62) - FOR COMPARISON

---

### Q87: What SMALL RPAS operations fall under Advanced Operations (Division V)?
**A:**
- VLOS in **controlled airspace**
- VLOS **16.4-100 feet** from uninvolved persons (any altitude)
- VLOS **less than 16.4 feet** from uninvolved persons (any altitude)
- VLOS within **3 NM of airport** or **1 NM of heliport**
- **Extended VLOS** in uncontrolled airspace
- **Sheltered operations**

---

### Q88: What MEDIUM RPAS operations fall under Advanced Operations (Division V)?
**A:**
- VLOS in uncontrolled airspace at **500+ feet** from uninvolved persons
- VLOS in uncontrolled airspace at **100-500 feet** from uninvolved persons
- VLOS at **less than 100 feet** from uninvolved persons
- VLOS in **controlled airspace**

---

### Q89: What distinguishes Advanced Operations from Level 1 Complex Operations?
**A:**
- **Advanced Operations (Division V)**: VLOS operations in various scenarios (controlled airspace, near people, near airports, extended VLOS, sheltered)
- **Level 1 Complex (Division VI)**: Specifically covers **BVLOS** operations in **uncontrolled airspace**

---

# SECTION 20: COMPARISON QUESTIONS

---

### Q90: Age requirements comparison - Basic vs Advanced vs L1C?
**A:**
| Certificate | Minimum Age |
|-------------|-------------|
| Basic | **14 years old** |
| Advanced | **16 years old** |
| Level 1 Complex | **18 years old** |

---

### Q91: What additional training is required for L1C beyond Advanced?
**A:**
- **20 hours of ground school instruction** specifically covering Level 1 Complex Operations (TP 15530)
- Pass the **L1C examination** (in addition to already having passed Advanced)

---

### Q92: Small vs Medium RPAS - weight ranges?
**A:**
| Category | Weight Range |
|----------|--------------|
| Small | **250g to 25 kg (55 lbs)** |
| Medium | **>25 kg to 150 kg (331 lbs)** |

---

### Q93: Small vs Medium RPAS - minimum VLOS distance from uninvolved persons?
**A:**
| Category | Minimum Distance |
|----------|------------------|
| Small | **100 feet (30 m)** |
| Medium | **500 feet (152.4 m)** |

---

### Q94: Small vs Medium RPAS - L1C BVLOS populated area rules?
**A:**
| Category | Populated Area Rule |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Medium** | MUST be **at least 1 km** from populated areas (Category a ONLY) |
| **Small** | Can be **1+ km** from populated areas (Category a) **OR less than 1 km/over sparsely populated** (Category b) |

**KEY POINT**: Medium RPAS have NO flexibility - they must always maintain 1 km. Small RPAS can operate closer.
