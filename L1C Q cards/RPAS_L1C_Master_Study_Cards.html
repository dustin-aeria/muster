PART 1: Core Definitions

Essential terminology for RPAS operations

Q1 Definitions
What is BVLOS?

Answer:

Beyond Visual Line-of-Sight - operating a remotely piloted aircraft outside the pilot's unaided visual range.

Q2 Definitions
What is VLOS?

Answer:

Visual Line-of-Sight - unaided visual contact maintained with a remotely piloted aircraft sufficient to:

  • Maintain control
  • Know location
  • Detect conflicting traffic and hazards
Q3 Definitions
What is Flight Geography?

Answer:

The area within which a remotely piloted aircraft is intended to fly for a specific operation during normal operations.

Q4 Definitions
What is Contingency Volume?

Answer:

The area immediately surrounding the flight geography within which contingency procedures are intended to be used to:

  • Return the aircraft to flight geography, OR
  • Safely terminate the flight
Key Point: Entry into contingency volume = abnormal situation
Q5 Definitions
What is Ground Risk Buffer?

Answer:

The area immediately surrounding the contingency volume that, when measured horizontally from the perimeter of the contingency volume, is at least equal to the planned maximum altitude.

Q6 Definitions
What is Operational Volume?

Answer:

The combined area composed of:

  1. Flight Geography
  2. Contingency Volume
  3. Ground Risk Buffer
Q7 Definitions
What is a Small Remotely Piloted Aircraft (sRPA)?

Answer:

Operating weight of at least 250g but not more than 25 kg (55 lbs).

Q8 Definitions
What is a Medium Remotely Piloted Aircraft (mRPA)?

Answer:

Operating weight of more than 25 kg (55 lbs) but not more than 150 kg (331 lbs).

PART 2: Level 1 Complex Operations (901.87)

BVLOS operations scope and requirements

Q9 L1C Operations
What two categories of BVLOS operations does Level 1 Complex (Division VI) cover?

Answer:

Category (a) - Small OR Medium RPAS:
  • BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
  • At least 1 km from a populated area (MANDATORY)
Category (b) - Small RPAS ONLY:
  • BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
  • Over a sparsely populated area, OR
  • Less than 1 km from a populated area
Q10 L1C Operations - CRITICAL
Can MEDIUM RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area under L1C?

Answer:

NO

Medium RPAS can ONLY conduct BVLOS under Category (a), which requires a minimum distance of 1 km from populated areas.

Category (b) allowing closer operations is restricted to small RPAS only.

Q11 L1C Operations - CRITICAL
Can SMALL RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area under L1C?

Answer:

YES

Small RPAS can operate under Category (b), which permits BVLOS:

  • Over sparsely populated areas, OR
  • At distances less than 1 km from populated areas
Q12 L1C Operations - KEY COMPARISON
What is the KEY difference between small and medium RPAS for L1C BVLOS operations?

Answer:

Aircraft Type Populated Area Rule
Medium RPAS Must ALWAYS maintain at least 1 km from populated areas (Category a ONLY)
Small RPAS Can operate at 1+ km (Cat. a) OR closer than 1 km / over sparsely populated (Cat. b)
KEY POINT: Medium RPAS have NO flexibility. Small RPAS CAN operate closer.

PART 3: Safety Assurance Declarations

CAR 901.194, Standard 922, PVD Process

Q13 Declarations
What is a Safety Assurance Declaration?

Answer:

A formal declaration made to Transport Canada by manufacturers, builders, or modifiers of RPAS that:

  • Identifies the capabilities of the system
  • Confirms it can perform advanced or complex operations safely
  • Certifies compliance with CAR 901.194 and Standard 922

Q15 Declarations
When is a Pre-Validated Declaration (PVD) required?

Answer:

  • Medium RPAS (25-150 kg) VLOS near people: Less than 152.5m (500ft) but not less than 30m (100ft)
  • Medium RPAS (25-150 kg) VLOS over people: Less than 30m (100ft)
  • Small RPAS (250g-25kg) BVLOS: Over sparsely populated area or less than 1 km from populated area

PART 4: JARUS SORA Methodology

Specific Operations Risk Assessment v2.5

Q17 SORA Fundamentals
What is SORA?

Answer:

Specific Operations Risk Assessment - A risk assessment methodology developed by JARUS to establish a sufficient level of confidence that a specific UAS operation can be conducted safely within the "Specific" category.

Q18 SORA - TLOS
What is the Target Level of Safety (TLOS) for ground risk?

Answer:

Less than 1 fatality per million flight hours (1E-6 fatalities/hour)

  2. Maximum speed of the UA
  3. Population density in the operational volume and ground risk buffer

PART 7: Calculations & Formulas

Volume and buffer calculations

Q24 Calculations
What is the formula for horizontal Contingency Volume (SCV)?

Answer:

SCV = SGNSS + SPos + SK + SR + SCM

Where:

  • SGNSS = GNSS accuracy (default 3m)
  • SPos = Position holding error (default 3m)
  • SK = Map error (default 1m)
  • SR = Reaction distance = V₀ × tR
  • SCM = Contingency maneuver distance
Q25 Calculations
What is the stopping distance formula for multirotors (SCM)?

Answer:

SCM = (1/2) × V₀² / (g × tan(θ))

Where:

  • V₀ = Speed (m/s)
  • g = 9.81 m/s²
  • θ = Bank angle (max 45°)

Requirements: thrust ≥ 2mg

Q26 Calculations
What is the simplified 1:1 rule for Ground Risk Buffer?

Answer:

SGRB = HCV + (1/2) × CD

Where:

  • HCV = Height of contingency volume
  • CD = Characteristic dimension of UA
