RPAS Level 1 Complex Operations
Comprehensive Study Flash Cards

Canadian Aviation Regulations (SOR/96-433) - Sections 901.62, 901.87, 901.194, 901.213

AUDITED & CORRECTED VERSION

SECTION 1: DEFINITIONS

Q1 What is BVLOS?
Beyond Visual Line-of-Sight - operating a remotely piloted aircraft outside the pilot's unaided visual range.
Q2 What is VLOS?
Visual Line-of-Sight - unaided visual contact maintained with a remotely piloted aircraft sufficient to:
  • Maintain control
  • Know location
  • Detect conflicting traffic and hazards
Q3 What is Extended VLOS?
An operation where the aircraft is NOT in visual line-of-sight, but unaided visual contact IS maintained with the airspace to detect conflicts and take avoidance action.
Q4 What is a Sheltered Operation?
An operation where the aircraft:
  • Remains less than 200 feet (61 m) horizontally from a building/structure
  • At an altitude no greater than 100 feet (30 m)
Q5 What is Flight Geography?
The area within which a remotely piloted aircraft is intended to fly for a specific operation.
Q6 What is Contingency Volume?
The area immediately surrounding the flight geography within which contingency procedures are intended to be used to:
  • Return the aircraft to flight geography, OR
  • Safely terminate the flight
Q7 What is Ground Risk Buffer?
The area immediately surrounding the contingency volume that, when measured horizontally from the perimeter of the contingency volume, is at least equal to the planned maximum altitude.
Q8 What is Operational Volume?
The combined area composed of:
  1. Flight Geography
  2. Contingency Volume
  3. Ground Risk Buffer
Q9 What is a Small Remotely Piloted Aircraft?
Operating weight of at least 250g but not more than 25 kg (55 lbs).
Q10 What is a Medium Remotely Piloted Aircraft?
Operating weight of more than 25 kg (55 lbs) but not more than 150 kg (331 lbs).
Q11 What is Operating Weight?
The weight of the aircraft at any point during flight, including:
  • Any payload
  • Any safety equipment aboard or connected
Q12 What is a Populated Area?
An area with more than 5 people per square kilometre.
Q13 What is a Sparsely Populated Area?
An area with more than 5 but not more than 25 people per square kilometre.
Q14 What is a Payload?
A system, object, or collection of objects (including slung loads) that is on board or connected to the aircraft but is NOT required for flight.
Q15 What is a Visual Observer?
A crew member who is trained to assist the pilot in ensuring the safe conduct of a flight.
Q16 What is a Fly-away?
When there is an interruption or loss of the command and control link such that the pilot is no longer able to control the aircraft.
Q17 What are Contingency Procedures?
Procedures to be followed to address conditions that could lead to an unsafe situation.

SECTION 2: LEVEL 1 COMPLEX OPERATIONS - SCOPE (901.87)

Q18 What two categories of BVLOS operations does Level 1 Complex (Division VI) cover?
Category (a) - Small OR Medium RPAS:
  • BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
  • At least 1 km from a populated area (MANDATORY)
Category (b) - Small RPAS ONLY:
  • BVLOS in uncontrolled airspace
  • Over a sparsely populated area, OR
  • Less than 1 km from a populated area
Q19 Can MEDIUM RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area?
NO - Medium RPAS can ONLY conduct BVLOS under Category (a), which requires a minimum distance of 1 km from populated areas. Category (b) allowing closer operations is restricted to small RPAS only.
Q20 Can SMALL RPAS conduct BVLOS operations closer than 1 km from a populated area?
YES - Small RPAS can operate under Category (b), which permits BVLOS:
  • Over sparsely populated areas, OR
  • At distances less than 1 km from populated areas
Q21 What is the KEY difference between small and medium RPAS for L1C BVLOS operations?
Aircraft TypePopulated Area Rule
Medium RPAS Must ALWAYS maintain at least 1 km from populated areas (Category a ONLY)
Small RPAS Can operate at 1+ km (Category a) OR closer than 1 km/over sparsely populated areas (Category b)
KEY POINT: Medium RPAS have NO flexibility - they must always maintain 1 km. Small RPAS can operate closer.
Q22 What airspace type is Level 1 Complex Operations restricted to?
Uncontrolled airspace ONLY - controlled airspace operations require additional certifications and ATC coordination.
Q23 Can you conduct Level 1 Complex Operations in controlled airspace?
NO - Level 1 Complex is restricted to uncontrolled airspace. Controlled airspace requires additional authorization.
Q24 Where can small RPAS conduct BVLOS under Category (b)?
In uncontrolled airspace:
  • Over sparsely populated areas (5-25 people/km²), OR
  • At a distance of less than 1 km from a populated area

SECTION 3: PILOT CERTIFICATE REQUIREMENTS

Q25 What is the minimum age for a Level 1 Complex Operations pilot certificate?
18 years old
Q26 What are the requirements for a Level 1 Complex pilot certificate?
  1. Be at least 18 years old
  2. Successfully complete the Advanced Operations examination (TP 15263)
  3. Complete 20 hours of RPAS ground school instruction from a training provider covering L1C operations (TP 15530)
  4. Pass the Level 1 Complex Operations examination (TP 15530)
  5. Complete flight review within 12 months prior to application (Standard 921)
Q27 What is the minimum age for a Basic Operations certificate?
14 years old
Q28 What is the minimum age for an Advanced Operations certificate?
16 years old
Q29 What ground school hours are required for Level 1 Complex?
20 hours of RPAS ground school instruction delivered by an approved training provider, covering subjects in TP 15530.
Q30 What examination must be passed BEFORE the L1C exam?
The Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems - Advanced Operations examination (TP 15263).
Q31 What Transport Canada documents define L1C knowledge requirements?
  • TP 15263: Basic and Advanced Operations knowledge requirements
  • TP 15530: Level 1 Complex Operations knowledge requirements

SECTION 4: RECENCY REQUIREMENTS

Q32 What is the recency period for Level 1 Complex Operations?
24 months - must have completed qualifying requirements within 24 months before operating.
Q33 What qualifies for recency under Level 1 Complex Operations?
Within 24 months, must have completed ONE of:
  • Issuance of the certificate, OR
  • Basic, Advanced, or L1C examinations, OR
  • Flight reviews per Standard 921, OR
  • Recurrent training activities per section 921.04 of Standard 921
Q34 How long must pilots retain recency activity records?
At least 24 months after the day on which they were completed.
Q35 What must be easily accessible during flight operations?
  • Pilot certificate
  • Proof of recency compliance
  • Authorization documents (if applicable)
  • Flight review completion records

SECTION 5: FLIGHT REVIEW & EXAMINATION RULES

Q36 How long before application must the flight review be completed?
Within 12 months prior to certificate application.
Q37 What is the waiting period to retake a failed examination?
24 hours before retaking failed examinations or flight reviews.
Q38 What is prohibited during examinations?
  • Cheating
  • Unauthorized assistance
  • Possession of unauthorized materials
Q39 What standard governs flight reviews for RPAS pilots?
Standard 921 - Remotely Piloted Aircraft, specifically section 921.02.

SECTION 6: RPAS OPERATOR CERTIFICATE

Q40 Is an RPAS Operator Certificate required for Level 1 Complex Operations?
YES - mandatory for conducting Level 1 Complex Operations.
Q41 Who can conduct Level 1 Complex Operations?
A person who:
  • IS an RPAS operator, OR
  • Is an employee, agent, mandatary, or representative of an RPAS operator
AND complies with the conditions in the RPAS operator certificate.
Q42 Who is eligible for an RPAS Operator Certificate (commercial)?
Must be Canadian (Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or Canadian-incorporated entity).
Q43 Who is eligible for an RPAS Operator Certificate (non-commercial)?
  • Canadian citizens or permanent residents
  • Government entities in Canada (or their agents/representatives)
  • Corporations incorporated under Canadian federal or provincial law
Q44 What information is required for Operator Certificate application?
  • Legal name and contact information
  • Accountable executive identification
  • Declaration of operations manual compliance
  • Training program compliance
  • Operational intent statement

SECTION 7: RPAS DECLARATION REQUIREMENTS (901.194)

Q45 What must be submitted in an RPAS Declaration?
  1. Legal name, trade name, address, contact details
  2. Model name of the RPAS
  3. Configurable elements of the system
  4. Intended operations to be conducted
  5. Technical requirements from Standard 922 subject to declaration
Q46 What makes an RPAS Declaration invalid?
Three conditions:
  1. The model fails to meet specified technical requirements
  2. The declarant notifies the Minister of invalidity
  3. Annual reporting requirements are not met
Q47 What document is required before making a declaration for certain advanced BVLOS operations?
An acceptance letter must have been issued within 2 years prior to making the declaration.
Q48 Within what timeframe must declarants notify the Minister of contact changes?
Within 30 days of any changes to names, addresses, or contact information.
Q49 What must be included in annual reports?
  • Operator name
  • Aircraft model name
  • Operational hours in Canada
  • Count of service difficulty reports with summaries of mandatory actions
Q50 Is a declaration required before conducting L1C operations?
YES - Per section 901.95, no pilot shall operate under Division VI (L1C) unless a declaration has been made in accordance with section 901.194.

SECTION 8-11: OPERATIONS MANUAL, SAFETY, TRAINING & RECORDS

Q51 What must the Operations Manual describe?
  • Crew roles and responsibilities
  • Personnel hierarchy
  • Established processes and procedures
  • Detailed training syllabus components
Q52 What documentation must operators provide to users?
  • Maintenance programs with servicing instructions and inspection procedures
  • Mandatory actions
  • Operating manuals covering: system descriptions, weight/CG limits, flight envelope, environmental effects, safety features, warning systems, emergency procedures, integration instructions
Q53 What safety processes must RPAS operators establish?
  • Setting aviation safety goals
  • Identifying hazards
  • Evaluating mitigation effectiveness
  • Reporting incidents
  • Ensuring maintenance compliance
Q54 What must RPAS operator training programs include?
  • Indoctrination training
  • Initial training (aircraft models, servicing/ground handling, operational procedures)
  • Annual recurrent training
Q55 How long must operator records be maintained?
12-24 months depending on record type.

SECTION 12: GENERAL OPERATING RULES

Q56 What is the standard maximum altitude for RPAS operations?
400 feet (122 m) AGL
Q57 What altitude restriction applies when within 200 feet horizontally of a structure?
Must maintain altitude no greater than 100 feet (30 m) above any building or structure.
Q58 What is the minimum horizontal distance from uninvolved persons for SMALL RPAS (VLOS)?
100 feet (30 m)
Q59 What is the minimum horizontal distance from uninvolved persons for MEDIUM RPAS (VLOS)?
500 feet (152.4 m)
Q60 What must pilots yield to?
Must give way to:
  • Power-driven heavier-than-air aircraft
  • Airships
  • Gliders
  • Balloons
AT ALL TIMES
Q61 What is the alcohol restriction for RPAS crew members?
Cannot serve:
  • Within 12 hours after consuming alcohol
  • While under alcohol's influence
  • While using impairing drugs
Q62 When must operations immediately cease?
If aviation safety or the safety of any person is endangered or likely to be endangered.
Q63 What must happen if an aircraft loses pilot control and enters/threatens controlled airspace?
Immediately notify the appropriate ATS unit or agency.
Q64 Can RPAS carry passengers?
NO - except with a special flight operations certificate.
Q65 Can RPAS leave Canadian Domestic Airspace?
NO - except with a special flight operations certificate.

SECTION 13: VISUAL OBSERVER REQUIREMENTS

Q66 What communication must be maintained between pilot and visual observer?
Reliable and timely communication during the operation.
Q67 Can a visual observer monitor multiple aircraft?
NO - cannot monitor multiple aircraft simultaneously (except under Division V or special certificates).
Q68 Can visual observers perform duties while operating vehicles?
NO - cannot perform duties while operating vehicles, vessels, or aircraft.
Q69 What certificates must visual observers hold?
Must hold one of:
  • Small RPAS basic certificate
  • Remotely piloted aircraft advanced certificate
  • Level 1 Complex Operations certificate
Q70 What is the maximum distance a visual observer can be from the aircraft?
Within 2 nautical miles of the aircraft.

SECTION 14: PRE-FLIGHT REQUIREMENTS

Q71 What must be determined in a site survey?
  • Airspace type and requirements
  • Altitudes and routes for approach/launch/recovery
  • Proximity of other aircraft operations
  • Distance from airports and aerodromes
  • Obstacles (wires, towers, buildings, turbines)
  • Weather and environmental conditions
  • Horizontal distance from uninvolved persons
  • Distance from populated areas (BVLOS)
Q72 What emergency procedures must be established?
  • Control station failure
  • Equipment failure
  • Aircraft failure
  • Loss of command and control link
  • Fly-away scenarios
  • Flight termination
  • Detection and avoidance of conflicting traffic

SECTION 15: CONTROLLED AIRSPACE & AIRPORT PROXIMITY

Q73 What is required for controlled airspace operations?
Authorization from air traffic services provider.
Q74 What distance restriction applies near airports?
Cannot operate within 3 nautical miles of airport centers without established safe-use procedures.
Q75 What distance restriction applies near heliports?
Cannot operate within 1 nautical mile of heliport centers without established safe-use procedures.

SECTION 16: REGISTRATION & NOTIFICATION

Q76 Within what timeframe must owners notify the Minister of aircraft changes?
Within 7 days of:
  • Aircraft destruction
  • Permanent withdrawal from use
  • Aircraft missing with terminated search
  • Aircraft missing 60+ days
  • Transfer of custody and control
